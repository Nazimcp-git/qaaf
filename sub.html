QAF — Quran Art Festival is a celebration of the sacred and the beautiful. It is a space where artists, reciters, poets, and designers gather under one vision: to express the timeless words of the Quran through the lens of art.

Born from the belief that creativity is an act of gratitude, QAF invites participants to channel their God-given talents in service of something greater than themselves. Every brushstroke, every note, every verse spoken is a form of remembrance.

Open to all age groups and skill levels, QAF is not merely a competition — it is a movement. A gathering of souls who believe that art and faith are not opposites, but mirrors of the same light.

"And We have certainly made the Quran easy for remembrance." — 54:17

Competition Programs

Seven disciplines
one language

Each program is a gateway — an invitation to express the Quran through a distinct artistic form.

01

Quranic Recitation

The art of tajweed brought to life. Participants perform selected verses with mastery of pronunciation, melody, and spiritual presence. Judged on maqam accuracy, emotional depth, and adherence to rules of recitation.

02

Arabic Calligraphy

The silent music of letters. Participants render Quranic verses in classical or contemporary calligraphic styles — Naskh, Thuluth, Diwani, or modern fusion. Evaluated on composition, technique, and spiritual resonance.

03

Nasheed & Vocal Arts

Sacred poetry in voice. Participants perform original or classical nasheeds drawing from Quranic themes. Solo and group categories. Criteria include vocal quality, lyrical depth, and the sincerity of expression.

04

Quranic Illustration

Painting the unseen. Artists create visual works inspired by Quranic verses, stories, or concepts. Any medium accepted — watercolour, acrylic, digital, ink. The work must visibly connect to a Quranic reference.

05

Quranic Photography

The lens as a vessel for meaning. Photographers submit images that embody a Quranic verse or concept. Judged on conceptual clarity, technical excellence, and the ability to evoke the verse without literal depiction.

06

Quranic Poetry & Creative Writing

Words that illuminate words. Participants compose original poems or short prose inspired by Quranic verses. Submissions may be in Arabic, English, or Malayalam. Evaluated on literary quality, imagery, and Quranic connection.

07

Quranic Graphic Design

Where tradition meets the digital age. Participants create poster designs, typographic compositions, or motion graphics rooted in Quranic verses. Assessed on visual communication, originality, and typographic mastery.
